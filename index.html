The Dream of My Life

Since childhood, I’ve always dreamed of becoming someone great in the field of computers. During my teenage years, I realised that my true ambition was to become a computer engineer 👨‍💻. Through consistent hard work 💪, calculated risks 🎲, and thoughtful decisions 🤔, I have built a successful career as a computer engineer with seven years of professional experience 🏆. Today, I hold a respectable position and take pride in being self-reliant in my profession 🌟.

However, this dream did not originate solely from within me — it has its roots in my childhood and, more deeply, in my father’s inspiration 👨‍👦. When I was in the 2nd or 3rd grade, my father introduced me to a computer for the first time 🖥️. He encouraged me to explore it, allowing me to play and experiment with digital painting 🎨. That early exposure ignited my curiosity 🔥 and shaped my future path.

My father’s first encounter with a computer was a moment of deep fascination 🤩. He was genuinely curious and eager to learn how it worked. But the environment around him wasn’t supportive 🚫. People warned him to be extremely careful ⚠️, saying that if he pressed the wrong button, he might spoil the computer 🤖. This fear-based advice discouraged him from exploring further. When he tried to learn from others, they refused to teach him 🙅‍♂️, saying they had taken formal courses and wouldn’t share their knowledge freely 📚. This left him feeling isolated in his curiosity, with no clear path to pursue his interest 😞.

Despite this, he never let go of his dream 🌠. Years later, when he saw my passion for technology 💡, he decided to give me the opportunity he never had. When I was in 4th grade, he bought me my first computer 🎁. He encouraged me to explore, learn, and grow without fear 🧑‍🏫. His support became the foundation of my journey into the world of computers—a journey he once wished to take himself.

I lost my father on September 4, 2025 💔, but his influence continues to guide me every day 🌅. Whatever I have achieved, and whatever I will accomplish in the future, is a reflection of his vision and support 🙏. My dream was never mine alone — it began as my father’s dream and lives on through me 🌟.
